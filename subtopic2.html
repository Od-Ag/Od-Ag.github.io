Abstract
 The article explains how the three-point seat belt, invented by Volvo engineer Nils Bohlin 60 years ago, has saved over one million lives and is one of the most important safety innovations. Volvo made the design available for free to all car makers to improve public safety. Today, the three-point seat belt is standard in cars, trucks, and buses.  The Evolution of the Airbag
Site Author
 Automotive Training Centre
Abstract
 This article explains the history and development of airbags in cars. Airbags were first invented in the 1940s, with early designs not working well. In the 1960s, improvements were made, and by the 1980s, airbags became more common in vehicles. Today, airbags are a key safety feature, including types like front, side, and seatbelt airbags. They help protect passengers in crashes and are now standard in many cars. Installing and replacing airbags is now a regular part of car repair training.
